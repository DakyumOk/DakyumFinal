How are Fintech startups in Canada similar to those in Italy, Spain and Portugal?

Italy

45% - a little less than half – of Italian FinTech companies are located in Milan. The City hosts more than 50,000 Financial services employees. 11,000 financial companies, including the Italian stock exchange. Within Fashion. 13,000 companies are operating in the sector. Around 100,000 people are employed, and turnover is exceeding 20 billion euros in 2019. Credimi, a popular Fintech company founded in 2015 in Milan, Italy focuses on Italian SMEs and is the biggest B2B digital leader of continental Europe. Credimi utilizes two proprietary algorithms “Octopus” and “JessicaF” which aid in the process to analyze and understand credit risks. Credimi’s liquidity is obtained from institutional investors in which the company claims is a process that creates a natural bridge between entrepreneurs and investors, a strong link between private savings and the real economy. Although interacting with the administration and moving in Italy may be challenging to foreigners, Credimi claims that Milan has improved as global ideologies are introduced.

Canada

Payfirma, a Canadian based company founded by Michael Gokturk in 2010 is a processing company allows customers to pay anywhere at any time with ease. Payfirma utilizes a cloud based platform to process transactions allowing businesses to access and retrieve data easily while also aiding in the process to accept online debit and credit transactions. Prior to the company receiving the Canadian Fintech Awards in 2015, the company received around $5.5 million in funding between 2011 to 2013. After receiving the award, Payfirm received a $13 million investment from Dundee Capital Markets.
